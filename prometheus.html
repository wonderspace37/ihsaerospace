Prometheus-Alpha

Published April 22, 2025

In the rapidly evolving world of space technology, agility, collaboration, and innovation are key. Prometheus-A embodies these principles, paving the way for groundbreaking space-based research while opening the door for others to ride alongside through our dedicated rideshare initiative, OrbitShare.

What is Prometheus-Alpha?

Prometheus-A is a multi-mission CubeSat platform designed to democratize access to low Earth orbit (LEO) through a reliable and modular rideshare system. Originally conceptualized as AetherSat, the mission has expanded in scope and ambition, now housing both internal research payloads and guest CubeSat modules through OrbitShare.

At the core of Prometheus-A is RAD-OES (Radio and Optical Environmental Surveyor), a pioneering science payload aimed at understanding the relationship between solar activity and radio frequency interference on Earth and in near-space environments.

Inside RAD-OES

Solar flares and coronal mass ejections (CMEs) emit high-energy particles that can severely disrupt radio communications, GPS, and satellite operations. RAD-OES aims to correlate real-time solar activity with fluctuations in RF environments, offering a clearer picture of how space weather affects global infrastructure.

Technical Specs of RAD-OES

  • Sensor Suite:
  • X-ray and UV photodiode system
  • Photon based particle detectors
  • Broadband RF Spectrum Analyzer
  • GPS based timing module
  • Onboard Processing
  • Edge computing for pre-filtering and flagging anomalous data
  • AI-assisted pattern detection to identify solar-induced interference
  • Mission Objective
  • Develop predictive models to forecast communication blackouts and anomalies
  • Provide open-source datasets for global scientific and aerospace communities

    • Join Us Through OrbitShare

    OrbitShare is our answer to high-cost, low-access space deployment. Through this program, Prometheus-A provides an open bay architecture for CubeSats and payloads under 3U to be integrated alongside the main mission.

    OrbitShare Highlights

  • Launch Vehicle: Compatible with major launch providers via standardized CubeSat dispensers
  • Orbit: Targeted Sun-synchronous orbit (SSO), approx. 500–600 km altitude
  • Integration Timeline: 4-month rolling window for partner onboarding, testing, and integration
  • Support: Comprehensive pre-launch technical reviews, thermal and vibration testing, and post-launch communication support
